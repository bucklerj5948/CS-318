What is Rocket League?

Rocket League is a fast-paced competitive videogame available on various platforms such as Xbox, PlayStation, Nintendo Switch, and PC. The game is equivalent to a quick-paced soccer game, but with cars! What better way to waste your time than playing intense matches of car soccer?!

Rules

Each traditional Rocket League match consists of a 5 minute time limit, and allows 2-6 players within each game, in either of the three matchup formats: Duel (1v1), Doubles (2v2), and Standard (3v3). There is no out of bounds as the entire field is encased in walls and round edges connecting to the floor, which allows infinite//limitless gameplay within the playing field. The objective of the game is to score more goals than the other team before the timer ends. If the time is up and the score is even, the two teams play off in an indefinite overtime that lasts until the next goal, in which the next goal wins.

Playlists

Within the game of Rocket League, there are two main playlists that are played amongst the majority of players; Casual and Competitive. Casual means you are put into matches that are for casual play, as there is no competitive rankings at stake. As for competitive, the games are no different from casual aside from the fact that they are competitive; competitive ranks are at stake, so if you lose//win, you will go down//up in rankings, which determines your actual skill within the game, and can be displayed amongst others' ranks from across the world; If you're good enough, you can even attend tournaments that reward thousands of dollars to the winning teams!
